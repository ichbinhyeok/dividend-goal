Strategy A vs Strategy B

Once you decide to save that money, where should it go? Here are two common paths for dividend investors:

Strategy A: The "Sleep Well" Approach (Stable)

Focuses on ETFs like SCHD or blue-chip Dividend Kings (e.g., KO, JNJ). You start with a lower yield (2-3%), but your income grows faster than inflation. This is for long-term wealth building.

Strategy B: The "Income Now" Approach (High Yield)

Focuses on Covered Call ETFs like JEPI or BDCs (e.g., MAIN, ARCC). You get high immediate income (7-10%), but less capital appreciation. This is great if you need cash flow today to pay bills.

Risks You Should Understand

  • Inflation Risk: If your dividends don't grow, your purchasing power drops. High-yield assets often have low growth.
  • Dividend Cuts: Companies can cut dividends. ETFs diversify this risk, but individual stocks can be dangerous.
  • Psychological Risk: Can you handle seeing your portfolio drop 20%? If not, you might sell at the bottom.

Who Is This Strategy For?

If you want to replace your salary eventually, Dividend Growth (Strategy A) is usually the winner. If you are already retired and need cash, High Yield (Strategy B) might make more sense.
